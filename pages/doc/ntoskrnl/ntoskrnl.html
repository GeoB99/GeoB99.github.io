 Kernel design summary

The NT kernel is the fundamental core at the deepest level in Windows that interacts with the bare metal (the hardware). The kernel compromises two distinct parts that makes up the whole NT kernel. The Executive and the microkernel (although the latter is more a microkernel-ish monolithic kernel than a very real microkernel per se, in rigorous definition of what a microkernel is). The Executive of the kernel bases on various kernel-mode modules of NTOSKRNL, the base kernel OS services such as memory manager, I/O manager, local procedural call (LPC) and a whole lot more.
